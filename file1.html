Famous Artists of the 21st Century

Banksy

Banksy is a world-renowned street artist whose real identity remains a mystery. Emerging from the UK graffiti scene in the 1990s, Banksy used stencils to create visually striking works, often with powerful social and political commentary. Fame came as his anonymous murals began appearing worldwide, capturing the imagination of critics and the public alike[web:6].

Notable Artworks:
  • Girl with Balloon
  • There Is Always Hope
  • Love Is in the Bin

Yayoi Kusama

Known as the Queen of Polka Dots, Kusama’s journey began in Japan, where strict family expectations clashed with her artistic ambitions. After moving to New York in the 1960s, she rose to fame for immersive installations that explore infinity, selfhood, and obsessive repetition. Her battle with mental illness directly inspires her richly imaginative works[web:1][web:2].

Notable Artworks:
  • Infinity Mirror Rooms
  • Dots Obsession
  • Flowers

Jeff Koons

Jeff Koons transformed kitsch and commercialism into celebrated art, often using new materials and production methods. Starting in advertising, Koons’s playful, shiny sculptures redefine what society considers fine art. His work stimulates debates about consumer culture and aesthetics, making him a central figure in contemporary art[web:2].

Notable Artworks:
  • Balloon Dog
  • Puppy
  • Rabbit

David Hockney

David Hockney is known for his vibrant depictions of domestic life and Southern California landscapes. Throughout a prolific career, he has embraced technology, working with iPads and photocopiers. Hockney’s iconic "Portrait of an Artist (Pool with Two Figures)" set records at auction, highlighting his enduring influence[web:7].

Notable Artworks:
  • Portrait of an Artist (Pool with Two Figures)
  • A Bigger Splash
  • Pacific Coast Highway and Santa Monica

Kerry James Marshall

Kerry James Marshall is celebrated for challenging dominant art narratives and promoting diversity. His paintings highlight Black identity and history, making him a transformative influence on the art world and helping open opportunities for underrepresented artists[web:3].

Notable Artworks:
  • Untitled (Studio)
  • Past Times
  • Vignette Series

Julie Mehretu

Mehretu’s abstract, large-scale paintings layer architecture, cartography, and dynamic forms to express themes of history, politics, and urban life. Her international recognition and innovative approach have inspired emerging artists and increased visibility for African diaspora creators[web:3].

Notable Artworks:
  • Stadia Series
  • Black City
  • Mural at Goldman Sachs
